How to Detect in Glaucoma

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve. The optic nerve sends visual information from your eye to your brain and is vital for good vision. Damage to the optic nerve is often related to high pressure in your eye. But glaucoma can happen even with normal eye pressure.

Data Collection: Measures intraocular pressure (IOP) to assess the risk of glaucoma. Elevated IOP is a common risk factor.
Preprocessing: Clean and preprocess the images to ensure consistency and enhance features. This may include resizing, normalization, and augmentation.
Model Selection: Choose a suitable machine learning model for object detection. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are commonly used for image-related tasks.
Training: Train your model using the labeled dataset. The model learns to identify patterns and features that distinguish one traffic sign from another.
Post-Processing: Depending on the requirements, you may need to post-process the results to improve accuracy or filter out false positives.

Several libraries and frameworks, such as TensorFlow and OpenCV, provide tools for building and deploying such computer vision models. Keep in mind that the accuracy of the model depends on the quality and diversity of the training data, as well as the complexity of the model architecture.
